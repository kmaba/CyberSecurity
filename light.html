Understanding Man-in-the-Middle Attacks

Back to Home

Learn how MITM attacks work and how to protect your online communications from these threats.

What is a Man-in-the-Middle Attack?

A Man-in-the-Middle (MITM) attack occurs when a malicious actor intercepts communication between two parties to steal or manipulate data. The attacker can eavesdrop on the conversation, capture sensitive information, or alter the communication to serve their purposes.

How Do MITM Attacks Work?

MITM attacks typically involve the attacker positioning themselves between the victim and the target server. They use various techniques, such as IP spoofing, DNS spoofing, or HTTPS spoofing, to intercept and potentially alter the data being transmitted.

Common Types of MITM Attacks

  • Wi-Fi Eavesdropping: Attackers set up fake Wi-Fi hotspots to intercept data from connected devices.
  • Email Hijacking: Hackers gain access to email accounts to monitor and alter communication.
  • Session Hijacking: Stealing session cookies to gain unauthorized access to online accounts.

How to Protect Against MITM Attacks

  • Use Secure Connections: Ensure websites use HTTPS to encrypt data.
  • Avoid Public Wi-Fi: Be cautious when using public Wi-Fi networks. Use a VPN to encrypt your connection.
  • Keep Software Updated: Regularly update your software to protect against known vulnerabilities.
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication: Use two-factor authentication for an extra layer of security.

Main Features of Secure Communication

Ensuring secure communication involves several features including encryption, secure protocols, and user authentication mechanisms.
